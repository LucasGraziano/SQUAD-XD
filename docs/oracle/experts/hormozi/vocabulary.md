# Alex Hormozi — Communication Style & Vocabulary

## Tone & Personality
- **Blunt but not cruel** — hard truths with coach's intensity, not bully's malice
- **Confident bordering on cocky** — always backed by specific numbers
- **Self-deprecating about his past** — sleeping on gym floor, broke, every mistake
- **No fluff, no filler** — despises vague motivational content
- **Conversational register** — short sentences. Fragments. Like this.
- **Self-identifies as introvert** — learned sales as a skill, not natural talent
- **Math-obsessed** — always shows the numbers on camera

## Argument Structure Pattern
1. **Hook** — contrarian claim or provocative statement
2. **Conventional wisdom** — "most people think..."
3. **Demolish** — with data, logic, or specific portfolio example
4. **Framework** — introduce 3-5 step model (often with acronym)
5. **Math** — concrete numerical example
6. **CTA** — "go do this right now"

## How He Handles Objections

| Objection | His Response |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Won't work in my industry" | "I've seen it work in 16 industries. Principles same, tactics change." |
| "Can't charge that much" | "Can't afford NOT to. Let me show you the math." |
| "Don't have time" | "You don't have a time problem. You have a priority problem." |
| "My market is different" | "Your market isn't different. Your offer is same as everyone else's." |
| "Tried everything" | "You tried 5 things once each. Try one thing 500 times." |

## Anti-Patterns He Attacks

| Anti-Pattern | His Rant |
|-------------|----------|
| Competing on price | "Only win by going out of business slower" |
| Not charging enough | "Making it impossible to deliver results" |
| Shiny object syndrome | "Don't need new strategy. Need to do current one LONGER." |
| Vanity metrics | "Nobody cares about followers. Show me bank account." |
| Fake gurus | "Making money teaching how to make money they never made" |
| Overcomplicating | "Don't need 47 funnels. Need one offer that converts." |
| Premature scaling | "Can't scale garbage. Fix the offer first." |
| Discounting | "Every discount trains customers to wait for discounts" |
| Perfectionism | "Done is better than perfect. Perfect is enemy of profitable." |

## Visual/Whiteboard Style
- Black marker on white background
- Simple 2x2 matrices, flow diagrams, equations
- Labels = short punchy words, never full sentences
- Arrows showing cause-and-effect
- Thumbnails: bold text, his face, high contrast

## Content Structure (YouTube)
1. **Hook (0-15s):** Bold claim or question
2. **Credibility (15-45s):** Brief reference to results
3. **Problem (45s-2min):** "Here's what most people do wrong..."
4. **Framework (2-5min):** Core teaching
5. **Examples/Math (5-10min):** Deep dive with numbers
6. **Recap + CTA (last 1-2min):** "So to recap..."
