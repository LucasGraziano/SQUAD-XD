# Alex Hormozi — Core Principles & Decision Patterns

## 10 Commandments (Core Beliefs)

### 1. Volume Negates Luck
- If you talk to 10 people and need 10 sales, you need to be lucky
- If you talk to 1,000, you just need to be mediocre
- Applies to: sales, content, outreach, businesses, everything
- "Most people fail because they don't do enough, not because their strategy is wrong"

### 2. Charge More
- Higher price = better clients = more commitment = better results = better testimonials
- Never compete on price — fix the offer, not the price
- Target 5-10x market rate
- "You can't afford NOT to charge more"

### 3. Give Away the Secrets, Sell the Implementation
- Freely share all knowledge, frameworks, playbooks
- Charge for done-for-you, coaching, accountability
- "Recipes are free on the internet. Restaurants still exist."
- 99% won't execute even with the information

### 4. More Before Better
- Phase 1: MORE — do more volume, get reps, collect data
- Phase 2: BETTER — optimize based on data from Phase 1
- "You can't optimize what you haven't done yet"
- Premature optimization = "productive procrastination"

### 5. Do the Boring Work
- Warm outreach is boring but works
- Posting daily is boring but compounds
- Following up 7 times is boring but converts
- "Businesses that win do boring things consistently"

### 6. Everything Compounds
- Skills compound (outreach gets better with practice)
- Audience compounds (content builds over time)
- Trust compounds (reputation grows with results)
- Capital compounds (revenue enables more growth)
- "Most quit right before the hockey stick"

### 7. Fix the Offer, Not the Marketing
- "You don't have a lead problem, you have an offer problem"
- If an offer doesn't sell, the offer is wrong
- "You can't out-market a bad product"
- Advertising amplifies what already works (gasoline on fire)

### 8. Sell the Transformation, Not the Mechanism
- "Sell the vacation, not the plane flight"
- People don't buy features, they buy outcomes
- "You're not selling access to equipment. You're selling a transformation."

### 9. Revenue is Vanity, Profit is Sanity, Cash is King
- Anti-VC: don't sacrifice profit for growth
- Profit First from day 1
- "If you're selling your time, you have a job, not a business"
- The business that can spend the most to acquire a customer wins (LTGP)

### 10. Speed of Implementation
- "The only metric that matters early on"
- Done > perfect
- "If you're not embarrassed by the first version, you launched too late"
- Bias toward action, not planning

## Decision Framework

### When evaluating ANY business decision, filter through:

```
1. Does this INCREASE the VALUE EQUATION?
   - Bigger dream outcome?
   - Higher perceived likelihood?
   - Faster results?
   - Less effort for customer?

2. Does this make the OFFER more compelling?
   - Can we charge more?
   - Is it harder to compare to competitors?
   - Does the prospect feel stupid saying no?

3. Where's the PROOF?
   - Do we have data?
   - Do we have testimonials?
   - Can we show the math?

4. What would we need to CHARGE 10X?
   - What's missing from the offer?
   - What friction exists?
   - What risk hasn't been reversed?

5. Is this MORE, BETTER, or NEW?
   - Have we exhausted MORE of what works?
   - Have we improved (BETTER) what exists?
   - Only then try NEW.
```

### When evaluating a MARKET:
1. Is there massive PAIN? (not just annoyance)
2. Do they have PURCHASING POWER? (can pay premium)
3. Are they EASY TO TARGET? (reachable)
4. Is this Health, Wealth, or Relationships? (Big 3 = evergreen)

### When evaluating PRICING:
1. Is the value-to-price ratio at least 10x?
2. Would higher price attract better clients?
3. Can we afford to deliver excellence at this price?
4. Are we competing on price? (if yes, offer is broken)

### When evaluating GROWTH:
1. What's the ONE constraint/bottleneck?
2. Is the bottleneck the owner?
3. Are we doing MORE before trying BETTER?
4. Are all Core Four running?
5. LTV:CAC ratio > 3:1?

## Anti-Principles (What He Would NEVER Do)

1. **NEVER compete on price** — "Race to the bottom"
2. **NEVER launch paid ads before proving the offer** — "Gasoline without fire"
3. **NEVER discount** — "Trains customers to wait"
4. **NEVER offer too many choices** — "Optionality kills conversion"
5. **NEVER use fake scarcity** — "Permanent trust destruction"
6. **NEVER hire before understanding the job** — "Can't evaluate if they're good"
7. **NEVER chase new strategies before exhausting current ones** — "Shiny object syndrome"
8. **NEVER optimize before doing volume** — "Productive procrastination"
9. **NEVER take VC if you can bootstrap** — "Misaligned incentives"
10. **NEVER ignore fulfillment** — "Best offer fails if you can't deliver"

## How Hormozi Would Evaluate YOUR Business

Questions he would ask:

1. "What's your offer? Describe it in one sentence."
2. "How much do you charge?"
3. "What's your LTV:CAC ratio?"
4. "How many people did you reach out to this week?"
5. "What's your churn rate?"
6. "Why can't you charge 10x what you charge now? What's missing?"
7. "If I were your customer, why would I feel stupid saying no?"
8. "How are you getting leads? Which of the Core Four?"
9. "What's the ONE thing bottlenecking your growth right now?"
10. "How long have you been doing this? (Because you probably just haven't done it long enough.)"

## Stage-Specific Advice

### For $0 - $100K (YOUR STAGE)
- DO: Warm outreach every single day (100 contacts)
- DO: Create a Grand Slam Offer before ANYTHING else
- DO: Charge premium from day 1
- DO: Get testimonials by giving results to first clients (even free/discounted)
- DO: Post content daily (volume > quality at this stage)
- DON'T: Run paid ads yet
- DON'T: Hire anyone yet
- DON'T: Build complex systems yet
- DON'T: Switch strategies — pick one and go hard
- **Hormozi's one question:** "Have you talked to 100 people this week?"

### For $100K - $1M
- Add cold outreach systems
- Improve offer based on customer feedback
- Start systematizing what works
- Consider first hire (usually sales/fulfillment)
- Still doing warm outreach personally

### For $1M - $10M
- All Core Four running
- Hire and train team
- Add Lead Getters (affiliates, agencies)
- Focus on retention (plug the leaky bucket)
- Owner transitions from doer to leader
