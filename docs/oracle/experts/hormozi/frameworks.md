# Alex Hormozi — Complete Framework Catalog

## From $100M Offers (2021)

### 1. The Value Equation (CORE)

```
                Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice
```

| Variable | Position | Description | To Increase Value... |
|----------|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Dream Outcome** | Numerator | The best possible result the prospect imagines | Increase — paint bigger, more vivid outcome |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | Numerator | How likely they believe they'll achieve the result | Increase — add proof, guarantees, testimonials |
| **Time Delay** | Denominator | How long until they get the result | Decrease — make it faster. Immediate > 6 months |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | Denominator | How much work/pain to achieve the result | Decrease — DFY > DWY > DIY |

**Key Insight:** Most compete only on Dream Outcome. The real leverage is the denominator — reducing time/effort toward zero makes value approach infinity.

### 2. Grand Slam Offer (6-Step Process)
1. **Identify Dream Outcome** — specific, not vague ("lose 20lbs in 6 weeks" not "lose weight")
2. **List ALL Problems/Obstacles** — before, during, and after purchase
3. **Turn Problems into Solutions** — each problem gets a corresponding solution
4. **Create Delivery Vehicles** — DFY (highest value) → DWY → DIY (lowest)
5. **Trim and Stack** — remove high-cost/low-value; keep low-cost/high-value
6. **Apply Enhancers** — scarcity, urgency, bonuses, guarantees

**Goal:** 10x value-to-price discrepancy minimum. Prospect cannot compare to competitors = can't commoditize you.

### 3. Pricing Principles

**Virtuous Cycle:**
```
Higher Price → More Client Investment → More Commitment → Better Results → Better Testimonials → More Demand → Higher Price
```

**Vicious Cycle:**
```
Lower Price → Worse Clients → Less Commitment → Worse Results → No Testimonials → Lower Demand → Even Lower Price
```

**Rules:**
- Charge 5-10x market rate
- 10x value-to-price discrepancy minimum
- Never compete on price — fix the offer, not the price
- "There is always someone willing to go out of business faster than you"

### 4. Guarantee Types

| Type | Description | Risk (Seller) |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Unconditional** | Full refund, no questions asked | High |
| **Conditional** | Refund IF they do X, Y, Z | Medium |
| **Anti-Guarantee** | "All sales final" (high demand only) | Low |
| **Implied** | Performance-based pricing | Variable |
| **Outcome-Based** | "No result = no pay" | Highest |
| **Better Than Money Back** | Refund + $500 for wasting their time | Very High |
| **Stacked** | Multiple guarantees layered | Varies |

**Principle:** Bigger guarantee = FEWER refunds (people commit more when they feel safe).

### 5. Bonus Stacking Rules
1. Each bonus addresses a SPECIFIC objection
2. Each has its own named value ("$2,000 value")
3. Total bonus value EXCEEDS core offer price
4. Low cost to deliver, high perceived value
5. Benefit-driven name, not feature-driven
6. Present 3-7 bonuses after core offer

### 6. MAGIC Naming Formula

| Letter | Element | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **M** | Magnetic Reason Why | "Back-to-School" |
| **A** | Announce Avatar | "Busy Moms" |
| **G** | Give Goal | "Belly Blaster" |
| **I** | Indicate Time Interval | "6-Week" |
| **C** | Complete with Container Word | "System" |

**Result:** "The Back-to-School Belly Blaster: 6-Week Rapid Shred System for Busy Moms"

### 7. Scarcity & Urgency (Ethical)

**Scarcity (Limited Supply):**
- Physical — limited seats/spots
- Analytical — "our team can only handle X/month"
- Personal — "I only work with X people"

**Urgency (Limited Time):**
- Cohort-based — "starts [date], next one in 3 months"
- Rolling deadlines — "expires 48h after you see it"
- Exploding bonuses — bonuses disappear, core offer remains

**Rule:** Must be REAL. Fake scarcity = permanent trust destruction.

### 8. Starving Crowd (Market Selection)

**3 Requirements:**
1. **Massive Pain** — problem that hurts badly
2. **Purchasing Power** — can afford premium
3. **Easy to Target** — reachable via ads/outreach

**Big 3 Evergreen Markets:** Health, Wealth, Relationships

### 9. Convergence
All 4 Value Equation variables maximized simultaneously:
- Dream Outcome = MASSIVE
- Perceived Likelihood = near CERTAIN
- Time Delay = near ZERO
- Effort/Sacrifice = near ZERO
= Can charge virtually any price.

## From $100M Leads (2023)

### 10. The Core Four (CENTRAL)

| | **Warm (Know You)** | **Cold (Don't Know You)** |
|---|---|---|
| **1-to-1** | Warm Outreach | Cold Outreach |
| **1-to-Many** | Post Free Content | Run Paid Ads |

**Rule:** Start with free methods (warm outreach + content), add paid methods when you have cash flow. Eventually run ALL FOUR simultaneously.

### 11. Warm Outreach — ACA Framework
1. **Ask** — about something in their life
2. **Compliment** — genuinely, specifically
3. **Ask** — soft transition to your offer

**The 100 Person Exercise:** Contact 100 people, DON'T pitch directly, ask "know anyone who has [problem]?"

**Script:** "Hey [Name], I'm starting [thing]. Looking for [number] people who want [dream outcome]. Doing it free/discounted for testimonials. Know anyone?"

### 12. Cold Outreach Formula
```
Results = Volume x List Quality x Offer Quality x Script Quality
```

**3 Elements:** Personalized opener → Case study/proof → Clear CTA

**Rule:** Follow-up is mandatory — most responses on follow-ups 3-7, not the first touch.

### 13. Content — Hook, Retain, Reward
- **Hook** — grab attention in 1-3 seconds
- **Retain** — story structure, open loops, pattern interrupts
- **Reward** — genuine value so they want more

**5 Content "Gives":**
1. Depict their problem back
2. Share personal journey
3. Teach frameworks/tactics
4. Showcase results/case studies
5. Share contrarian opinions

### 14. Paid Ads Framework
```
Profit = (Revenue/Customer x Customers) - Ad Spend - Fulfillment Cost
```

**Rule:** LTV > 3x CAC minimum. "Ads are gasoline — if you have a fire (working offer), it makes it bigger. If you don't, you waste money."

### 15. Lead Magnet — 7 Characteristics
1. Solves a narrow, specific problem
2. Provides a "complete" solution to that narrow problem
3. Delivers value immediately
4. Easy to consume
5. High perceived value
6. Creates awareness of a bigger problem (that your paid offer solves)
7. Naturally leads to your core offer

### 16. More → Better → New (Scaling Priority)

| Priority | Action | Risk |
|----------|--------|------|
| **1st: More** | Do more of what works | Lowest |
| **2nd: Better** | Improve what you're doing | Medium |
| **3rd: New** | Try new channels/methods | Highest |

**Rule:** Exhaust "More" before "Better." Exhaust "Better" before "New."

### 17. Lead Getters (4 Types)
1. **Customers** → referrals/word-of-mouth
2. **Employees** → trained on Core Four
3. **Agencies** → Core Four on your behalf
4. **Affiliates** → their audience sends you leads

### 18. Advertising Lifecycle

| Phase | Status | Methods | Goal |
|-------|--------|---------|------|
| 1. Startup | No money, have time | Warm outreach + Content | First customers |
| 2. Growth | Some money | + Cold outreach | Predictable leads |
| 3. Scale | Have money | + Paid ads, scale teams | Volume + efficiency |
| 4. Leverage | Have systems | All 4 + Lead Getters | Leads without your effort |

### 19. LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)
```
LTGP = (Avg Revenue/Customer x Avg Purchases) - Fulfillment Cost
```

**Key:** Business that can spend the most to acquire a customer WINS. High LTGP = can lose money on first sale and still profit.

### 20. Give-to-Ask Ratio
- Early relationship: Give, give, give, give, give... ask
- Trust built: Give, give, ask
- Loyal customer: Give, ask

## From Gym Launch Secrets (2019)

### 21. CLOSER Framework (Sales)

| Letter | Step | Script |
|--------|------|--------|
| **C** | Clarify | "What made you come in today?" |
| **L** | Label | Reflect their problem with specificity |
| **O** | Overview | "What have you tried? Why didn't it work?" |
| **S** | Sell the Vacation | Paint the "after" picture, not the process |
| **E** | Explain Away | Handle objections with empathy + logic |
| **R** | Reinforce | Confirm they made a great choice. Prevent buyer's remorse. |

### 22. 6-Week Challenge Model (Front-End Offer)
- Specific transformation in 6 weeks
- Money-back if they complete and hit goal
- Gym keeps money if they don't complete
- Converts to long-term membership

### 23. Four Cash Flow Machines
1. **Front-End Offer** — brings new customers in
2. **Core Offer** — main ongoing service
3. **Upsells/Cross-sells** — additional products for existing customers
4. **Reactivation** — bringing back former customers

### 24. PIF vs Payment Plans
- **Paid-in-Full:** Better cash flow, higher commitment, lower LTV risk
- **Payment Plan:** Slower, higher drop-off risk
- **Strategy:** Offer significant discount for PIF (e.g., $3K PIF vs $4K over 6 months)

## Cross-Book Principles

### 25. Three Ways to Grow a Business
1. Get MORE customers (leads + conversion)
2. Increase AVERAGE ORDER VALUE (pricing, upsells)
3. Increase PURCHASE FREQUENCY (retention, reactivation)

Growing each by 20% = 1.2 x 1.2 x 1.2 = **72.8% total growth**

### 26. The Entrepreneur's Equation
```
Profit = (Units Sold x Price) - COGS - OpEx
```
**Hormozi's lever:** Increase Price by increasing Value (more effective than selling more units).
